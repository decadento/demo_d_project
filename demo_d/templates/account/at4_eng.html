AT-4 hand grenade launcher.

How hand grenade launchers are able to stop vehicles during an attack. When enemy vehicles attack infantry units or positions, AT-4 grenade launcher operators can react quickly, delivering precision strikes on targets with great speed and accuracy.

May 19. Author: decadent

The reason for appearing on the battlefield

The history of the AT-4 grenade launcher is connected with the beginning of the Cold War and the need to improve anti-tank weapons. The development of this system began in the 1970s in Sweden, when there was a need for more effective and portable anti-tank weapons for military units. The company Saab Bofors Dynamics took up this task and after several years of development released the first AT-4 model in 1980.

The first modifications of the grenade launcher were successfully tested and put into service with the troops. Over the following decades, the system underwent several upgrades and improvements to meet the changing needs of troops and modern combat conditions. Today, the AT-4 grenade launcher remains one of the key components of the equipment of many armies of the world and is used both for defense and for attacking armored targets.

General description

The AT-4 grenade launcher is capable of effectively fighting armored targets, such as light tanks, armored personnel carriers and other armored vehicles. Its compactness and portability make it ideal for use in urban environments or in confined spaces where it is difficult to maneuver larger anti-tank systems.

Being a disposable model, it does not require recharging and can be quickly used in a combat situation.

The AT-4 grenade launcher is also known for its reliability and ease of use. Its design allows even small military units to carry out effective anti-tank defense without the need for specialized skills or extensive training. This makes it popular with many armies around the world looking for a simple and effective means of countering armor.

Characteristics

The AT-4 grenade launcher has a built-in hydrodynamic fuse, which allows it to penetrate enemy armor and inflict significant damage. Its easy-to-use system allows for quick set-up and aiming, making it ideal for short-range shooting in active combat environments. Although its range is limited compared to heavier anti-tank systems, it still plays an important role in combat operations.

The AT-4 grenade launcher can also be used to destroy fortifications and other objects that require significant firepower to destroy. Its lightness and compactness make it an attractive option for transport and use in environments where speed and mobility are important.

  • Caliber: 84 mm.
  • Firing range: From 20 to 300 meters.
  • Weight without ammunition: Approximately 6.7 kilograms.
  • Ignition system: Hydrodynamic ignition.
  • Purpose: Anti-tank weapon for destroying armored targets.

Instructions for use

Before using the AT-4 grenade launcher, the operator must also check the condition of the weapon, including its integrity and presence of malfunctions. It is necessary to ensure that the sighting device is correctly installed and adjusted, as well as that the ammunition is installed correctly.

After using the AT-4 grenade launcher, it is necessary to keep records of the consumption of ammunition and notify higher officials about the need to replenish stocks.
