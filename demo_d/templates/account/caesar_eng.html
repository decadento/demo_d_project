SPG CAESAR

The French CAESAR self-propelled gun in the steppes of Ukraine: the infantry and armored vehicles of the occupiers were destroyed. Technologies and tactics reflect the combination of strategic planning and military power that are key in today's era of conflict.

May 19. Author: decadent

CAESAR

The CAESAR (CAmion Equipé d'un Système d'ARtillerie), French for "truck equipped with an artillery system", is a wheeled 155mm self-propelled howitzer developed in France by arms manufacturer GIAT Industries, now known as Nexter. Self-propelled guns are designed to destroy the enemy's manpower, artillery, fortifications and other equipment.

The artillery installation is equipped with a modernized version of the 155-mm TRF1 gun with a barrel length of 52 calibers, mounted in the rear part of the wheeled chassis. Transportable ammunition - 18 shells. Rate of fire - 6 shots per minute. CAESAR is capable of using the entire range of NATO 155-mm artillery shells, including high-precision GPS-type Excalibur or Vulcano.

As the chassis of the self-propelled guns for the French army, the Renault Sherpa 5 with a wheel formula of 6x6 is used, which has an armored cabin that protects the gun crew of 5 soldiers from shell fragments and shelling with 7.62 mm bullets. There is an air conditioning system. The weight of the installation is about 18 tons, the length is 10 m, the width is 2.55 m.

CAESAR self-propelled guns are equipped with navigation devices, aiming devices and communication systems. The artillery installation uses the SIGMA 30 fire control system, which includes a receiver of the NAVSTAR navigation system, a radar for measuring the initial velocity of the projectile and a built-in ballistic calculator. The ACS is also equipped with a mechanized projectile delivery system.

CAESAR was created to replace the TRF1 trailed howitzers in the French army, and later the AUF1 self-propelled guns. The first sample of the new ACS was shown in 1994. In 2000, a contract was signed for the supply of five pre-production units on the Mercedes-Benz Unimog U2450L (6x6) chassis to the French army. Self-propelled guns were used for familiarization with the new artillery system and for experimental operation in the troops.

In 2004, they signed a contract for the supply of 72 CAESAR self-propelled howitzers to the French ground forces, but already on the domestic Renault (Arquus) Sherpa 5 (6x6) chassis. Serial production of new self-propelled guns was established in 2007. But at the request of the customer, the artillery system can be integrated on different chassis.

Currently, the CAESAR modification is available on the Tatra 815-7 (8x8) wheeled chassis, which was ordered by Denmark and the Czech Republic.

The National Guard of Saudi Arabia also has more than 70 CAESAR self-propelled guns, which are manufactured in the country under a French license. They are based on the Mercedes-Unimog U2450L (SOFRAME-UNIMOG) chassis with a 6x6 wheel formula.

In February 2024, the French military ordered 109 CAESAR 155 mm self-propelled howitzers in the new MkII version, which was under development from December 2021. Belgium and Lithuania also became customers for this modification of French self-propelled guns.

The updated self-propelled installation retained the main features of the CAESAR self-propelled gun, which is already in service on the Arquus Sherpa 5 (6×6) wheeled chassis. The main efforts are aimed at improving crew protection and increasing mobility. For this purpose, a new armored cabin with anti-mine and ballistic protection, developed by Nexter, will be installed on the new version of the ACS. Also, the company Arquus (former Renault Trucks Defense) completely redesigned the chassis of the car.

The CAESAR MkII is equipped with a new 460 hp diesel engine, a new automatic transmission and a new chassis. CAESAR will also receive a fire control system with the most modern version of the software, as well as a new Thales CONTACT radio station. It is also possible to install a mine protection system on the ACS - the Thales BARAGE radio suppression system for the signals of explosive devices.

CAESAR in Ukraine

On April 22, 2022, it became known about the transfer of CAESAR self-propelled guns to Ukraine as part of military aid from France. At that time, President Emmanuel Macron said in an interview that he supplies Ukraine with a variety of weapons "from Milans (Milan ATGMs - "Military") to Caesars" and plans to do so in the future. Ukrainian gunners were trained on them.

And already in May it became known that the French CAESAR wheeled 155-mm self-propelled artillery installations are already on the territory of Ukraine and are being used against the Russian invaders.

The Ukrainian military also showed photos of crews and provided foreign self-propelled guns, which are already in use on the front lines.

Already in the summer, Ukrainian gunners showed journalists how the control system of CAESAR self-propelled guns works, which fired at targets in the rear of Russian troops in Donbas.

The military emphasized that one of the main advantages of self-propelled guns is a digital fire control system, which allows you to fire almost immediately after occupying a position.

Gunners can enter the coordinates of the target while moving into position, and the system does everything by itself.

Artilleryman Gleb said that the task of the crew is only to enter the coordinates. "I can do it at least while advancing to the position, and the system will already calculate all the necessary data for hitting the target."

The military also added that after all the calculations, the required ammunition is selected. CAESAR self-propelled artillery can complete a combat mission in 3-5 minutes. This time includes taking up the firing position and opening fire.

This allows Ukrainian gunners to work according to the principle of "maneuver-fire-maneuver", which involves a constant change of position. Drones are used to adjust fire. CAESAR self-propelled gunners are in constant contact with aerial reconnaissance.

Ukraine received several batches of CAESAR from France in 2022-2023. Artillery installations demonstrated high efficiency on the battlefield with the Russian invaders. Ukraine also received 19 self-propelled howitzers, but on the Tatra 815-7 8x8 chassis from Denmark.

CAESAR in this version has an armored cabin and a gross weight of 32 tons, equipped with a Tatra V8 diesel engine with a capacity of 410 hp. Service is reduced to three people. An ammunition set of 30 shots is placed on the installation.

The transfer of these artillery systems was accompanied by the training of future Ukrainian crews.

Increase in production

The French defense company Nexter has increased the pace of production of CAESAR self-propelled guns. The production cycle was cut in half: from 30 to 15 months.

In January 2024, the French Minister of Defense Sebastien Lecornu assured that the increased pace of production will make it possible to release almost 80 artillery installations in 2024. This will become possible thanks to the reduction of the production cycle of one ACS from 30 to 15 months. In addition, the Nexter manufacturer was able to create the necessary stocks and hire additional workers.

In addition, the Franco-German defense company KNDS, which includes Nexter, predicted an increase in demand for CAESAR self-propelled guns. The manufacturer announced the reorganization of its production lines. Thanks to this, the output should increase from the current 6 howitzers to 8 per month in 2024.

New artillery systems are needed both to continue supplies to the Ukrainian Defense Forces and foreign customers, and for the French military to replace those artillery installations that were given to Ukraine as defense aid.

Artillery coalition

On January 18 of this year, the Minister of Defense of France, Sebastien Lecornu, together with his Ukrainian colleague, Rustem Umyerov, launched an international artillery coalition for Ukraine.

The initiative aims to provide the Ukrainian Armed Forces with artillery weapons in the short and long term. Weapons are necessary to strengthen resistance to Russian aggression. Also, the coalition will help Ukraine ensure the effective functioning of already provided samples of artillery weapons. Representatives of 22 countries attended the first meeting held in Paris.

Within the framework of the project, it is planned to purchase 78 CAESAR artillery installations for Ukraine, which should be manufactured in 2024. It is assumed that each of the countries participating in the coalition will contribute to the financing of the project. Thus, France will use 50 million euros from its Ukraine Support Fund to order 12 of the 78 howitzers. Production of 6 more self-propelled guns was financed by Ukraine.
